Project 02 · Research

Generative AI research — from idea to patent.

Two applied research threads using generative AI: one personalizing educational content, one tackling perception for autonomous driving. Both were presented publicly, recognized nationally, and turned into patent filings — and one into a published SPIE paper.

RoleResearcher & Author
OutputSPIE paper · 2 patents

DomainsEducation · Autonomy

Thread 01 · Education

Personalizing learning with generative AI.

This research explored how generative AI can tailor educational content to individual learners — the ideas that also power Neorons. I built and evaluated the approach, then presented it as an in-person finalist at JSHS (the Junior Science & Humanities Symposium).

The work went beyond a demo: it became a filed patent, formalizing the method for adapting content to a learner's needs.

  • Generative AI personalization
  • Multi-step reasoning
  • JSHS Finalist & presenter
  • Patent filed

Thread 02 · Autonomy

Generative AI for autonomous driving.

In a second thread, I applied generative AI to challenges in autonomous driving. The research earned the SPIE Young Author Award and was published in SPIE — a peer-reviewed venue — with a related patent filing.

Together, the two threads show a consistent method: take a hard real-world problem, apply generative and probabilistic models, validate the result, and protect and publish the contribution.

  • Generative AI
  • Probabilistic decision models
  • Deep learning (PyTorch)
  • SPIE Young Author Award
  • Published + patent
